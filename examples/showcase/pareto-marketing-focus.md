# Showcase — `pareto-principle` (standalone)

## Input

> Apply 80/20 to: Our marketing team has 23 active initiatives. Pipeline is below target. Team is burned out. Where should we focus?

## Output (verbatim)

> **The vital 20%:** Three initiatives likely drive the majority of pipeline impact: (1) the highest-probability-of-close opportunities currently in qualified pipeline, (2) the top 2–3 channels historically responsible for the largest revenue contribution, and (3) feature work directly unblocking stalled deals in late-stage negotiations.

**Actively deprioritized (the 80%):**
- Experimental channels with unproven ROI
- Early-stage awareness campaigns (full-funnel without pipeline pressure)
- Exploratory feature work not tied to current deal motions
- Internal process optimization projects not blocking revenue
- Competitive intelligence gathering and market research
- Brand and thought-leadership content initiatives
- Testing new vertical/segment expansion
- Platform migrations or marketing-stack consolidation projects

## What to notice

- **3 items in the vital 20%** — within the 1–4 default range.
- **8 deprioritized items.** Several are real concerns (brand work, vertical expansion, platform migrations) — that's the discipline. Naming legitimate work as "not now" is harder than picking obvious filler.
- **Specific framing.** "Highest-probability-of-close opportunities currently in qualified pipeline" — the user could literally pull a CRM list filtered that way and start.
- The output assumes the user has data to operationalize ("the top 2–3 channels historically responsible") — if they don't, that's the next step.
