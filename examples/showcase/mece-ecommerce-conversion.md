# Showcase — `mece-framework` (standalone)

## Input

> Categorize the factors that could explain why the conversion rate on our outdoor-gear DTC e-commerce site dropped from 4.1% to 2.7% over Q3. Don't analyze, don't drill, don't recommend — just categorize.

## Output (verbatim)

- **Traffic & Visitor Volume**
  - Overall site traffic decline
  - Paid acquisition drop
  - Organic search visibility loss
  - Direct traffic loss

- **Traffic Quality & Source Mix**
  - Attribution model changes
  - Bot or low-intent traffic increase
  - Shift toward lower-intent channels

- **Product & Inventory**
  - Stock outs on best-sellers
  - Product quality or fit issues
  - Price increases
  - New product underperformance
  - Assortment misalignment with demand

- **Checkout & Technical**
  - Site speed degradation
  - Mobile experience issues
  - Payment processing errors
  - Cart abandonment friction increase
  - Technical bugs or broken flows

- **Competitive & Market**
  - Competitor pricing changes
  - Competitor marketing campaigns
  - Lost exclusive partnerships or brands
  - Market saturation or customer shift

- **Customer Experience & Trust**
  - Shipping cost or time changes
  - Return policy tightening
  - Review quality decline
  - Brand reputation damage
  - Customer service degradation

- **Pricing & Promotion**
  - Discount or promo reduction
  - Price increase
  - Removal of incentives (free shipping, etc.)
  - Less attractive loyalty program

## What to notice

- 7 categories — slightly above the 3–6 default. Defensible for a multi-channel DTC business with both site and external dependencies, but a critic could argue Customer Experience and Pricing are partially overlapping with Product and Traffic Quality.
- Sub-factors are concrete and testable (e.g., "stock outs on best-sellers" can be queried directly).
- Output is just the bullet structure — no preamble, no analysis. That's the skill working as designed.
