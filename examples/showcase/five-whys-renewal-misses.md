# Showcase — `five-whys` (standalone)

## Input

> Drill from this surface problem to root cause: Our customer success team is missing 40% of their renewal-call commitments to customers, and customer health scores are dropping as a result.

## Output (verbatim)

**Starting problem:** Our customer success team is missing 40% of their renewal-call commitments to customers, and customer health scores are dropping as a result.

1. **Why?** Customer success reps are not following through on renewal-call commitments they made during initial customer interactions.

2. **Why?** There is no centralized tracking system for renewal-call commitments, so reps lose visibility of which customers they committed to contact and when.

3. **Why?** The team relies on scattered individual calendars and CRM notes rather than a unified renewal-call tracker, making it difficult to surface upcoming commitments across the team.

4. **Why?** There is no renewal-call process owner and no scheduled cadence to review pending commitments or escalate at-risk renewals before the call window passes.

5. **Why?** The customer success operations function lacks process management discipline — there is no documented renewal workflow, no accountability structure, and no SLA enforcement around commitment tracking.

**Root cause:** Customer success operations has no dedicated process owner for renewal-call management and no systematic workflow to track, prioritize, and enforce completion of renewal commitments, creating a gap between what reps promise and what gets delivered.

## What to notice

- **Each Why builds on the previous.** The chain progresses from "reps don't follow through" → "no tracking" → "scattered systems" → "no process owner" → "no operational discipline."
- **Stops at a STRUCTURAL cause.** Not "reps are lazy" or "customers are demanding" — but "no process owner, no documented workflow, no SLA enforcement." That's something you can fix as a system change.
- **Root cause is one sentence and actionable.** A leader could read this and immediately know what to commission: a process owner role + a renewal-call tracking system with SLAs.
