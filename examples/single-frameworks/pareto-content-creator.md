# Pareto Principle — Content creator / YouTube

A standalone run of the `pareto-principle` skill on a content-creator growth problem.

---

## Input (what the user typed)

> I'm a YouTuber with 47K subscribers across three content pillars: AI tutorials, business strategy, and productivity. I publish 2x/week and I'm stretched thin. Looking at the last 12 months: subscribers are up 40%, but I have no idea which content is actually doing the work. I need to focus.

---

## Output (what the skill produced)

> **The vital 20%:** Two content categories likely drive ~80% of subscriber growth based on the typical creator pattern in this niche: (1) **AI tutorials with a specific tool integration** (e.g., "Claude + [tool]" or "ChatGPT + [workflow]") — these dominate search-driven discovery and tend to show 5–10× the subscriber-conversion rate of pure commentary. (2) **Business-strategy videos that unpack a famous case study or framework** with a strong thumbnail hook. Together, an estimated 6–10 hero videos per year carry the bulk of net-new subscriber growth, while everything else functions as retention/cadence content. Verify by pulling YouTube Studio's "subscribers gained" by video and seeing if the top 10 videos account for ≥70% of net-new subs over 12 months.

**Actively deprioritized (the 80%):**
- General productivity content (overcrowded niche, low search intent)
- Newsy or react-style videos on AI announcements (decay fast, low SEO half-life)
- Long-form interviews or solo monologues without a tool/case-study hook (high effort, low subscriber lift)
- Shorts as a primary growth channel (good for reach, weak for sub conversion in this niche)
- Cross-platform repurposing (LinkedIn, TikTok) until YouTube cadence is sustainable
- Membership / Patreon launch (premature — subscriber base too small for meaningful conversion)
- Sponsorship outreach (right idea, wrong timing — focus on growth first)

---

## What to notice

- **Vital 20% has 2 items.** Default range is 1–4. Two is right for a creator with three pillars — naming one would be too narrow, naming all three would defeat the framework.
- **The vital 20% includes a verification step.** Without YouTube Studio data, this is an informed hypothesis. The skill names exactly what to check ("top 10 videos for ≥70% of net-new subs") so the user can confirm before committing.
- **Deprioritized list contains things that genuinely matter.** Shorts, cross-platform, and Patreon are real growth levers — but they're explicitly named as not-yet. That's the discipline. The user has six legitimate distractions; the framework's job is to call them distractions for now.
- **Productivity pillar is named for elimination.** That's the hardest move. The user is currently producing content there but the data (and the niche dynamics) suggests it's the lowest-leverage of the three pillars. The blockquote commits to this even though it might feel like sunk-cost loss.
- **Sequencing:** once the user pulls Studio data, this should be re-run with actual numbers. If it confirms, the next framework is `so-what-test` to translate "focus on AI tutorials + case studies" into a concrete content calendar with named series and a 90-day target.
